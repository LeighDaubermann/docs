## Predictive Staffing

Woblar's staffing agent connects to your POS system and builds a demand-forecasting model tailored to your venue or store. It then uses that model to generate optimised rosters automatically — no manual scheduling required.

Here's how the agent builds and delivers your roster:

- **Analyses historical POS transaction data** — the agent studies your sales patterns by day, time, and season to understand exactly when you're busy and when you're not.
- **Overlays local weather forecasts** — because footfall in a café or restaurant is directly affected by the weather, the agent factors in the forecast for your area.
- **Incorporates community events calendars** — local sports fixtures, concerts, public holidays, and school holidays all affect demand. The agent accounts for them automatically.
- **Predicts staffing requirements by day and hour** — forecasts achieve 85%+ accuracy, so you're never caught under-staffed on a busy Friday night or paying for idle staff on a quiet Tuesday morning.
- **Delivers optimised rosters directly to your manager's phone** — the roster lands as a ready-to-share schedule, not a data dump. Your manager reviews and sends — the heavy lifting is already done.

## Predictive Inventory

For e-commerce and warehouse businesses, Woblar connects to your ERP and replaces manual spreadsheet forecasting with a bespoke predictive model that adjusts automatically as conditions change.

The inventory agent does the following:

- **Ingests your ERP purchase history and supplier lead times** — the model understands your real procurement cycle, not a generic industry average.
- **Incorporates external signals** — weather patterns, social trends, and seasonal demand shifts feed into the forecast alongside your internal data.
- **Autonomously adjusts reorder points** — as predicted demand changes, the agent updates reorder thresholds so your stock levels move with the market, not behind it.
- **Drafts purchase orders before a stockout occurs** — the agent generates draft POs for your review before you run out, giving you time to approve rather than scramble.
- **Frees trapped capital by preventing over-ordering** — slow-moving SKUs get flagged before you re-order them unnecessarily. Your cash flow improves without you having to monitor individual SKUs manually.

<Note>
  Woblar builds bespoke predictive models tailored to your specific business — your product mix, your trading patterns, your suppliers. There is no off-the-shelf generalisation applied to your data.
</Note>

## Setup Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Share your POS or ERP platform details with Woblar">
    Let the team know which system you use — whether that's a major platform like Lightspeed, MYOB, SAP, or a custom-built solution — so they can plan the right integration approach.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Woblar connects to your data source">
    The team sets up a secure API connection to your POS or ERP and begins ingesting your historical data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Woblar trains the bespoke predictive model">
    Using your historical data alongside external signals, the team builds and validates a forecasting model calibrated to your business.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Woblar delivers the first forecast for your review">
    Before going fully live, you review the first roster or inventory forecast to confirm accuracy and alignment with your operational expectations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go live — forecasts run automatically">
    Staffing rosters and inventory adjustments are generated and delivered on schedule without any manual input from your team.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  This integration requires a minimum of 3 months of historical POS or ERP data to train accurately. The more historical data you can provide, the sharper the forecasts will be from day one.
</Info>

<Tip>
  Share your peak trading periods and known event calendar with Woblar during setup — local festivals, school holidays, public holidays, and major sporting events. Feeding this context in early improves forecast accuracy significantly from the moment you go live.
</Tip>
